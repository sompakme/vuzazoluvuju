The Tenors (formerly known as The Canadian Tenors) are a vocal quartet comprised of Remigio Pereira, Victor Micallef, Fraser Walters, and Clifton Murray who perform as a group throughout the United States and Canada. They play operatic pop music that is a fusion of classical and popular music, including songs such as “The Prayer” and Panis Angelicus from the classical genre and Leonard Cohen’s Hallelujah from the pop genre among their repertoire. Pereiera is from Ottawa/Gatineau, while Micallef is from Toronto. Both players are from Canada. Walters, from Vancouver, and Murray, from Port McNeil are among the participants. The Tenors have appeared on more than 60 worldwide television shows during their careers. They appeared on The Oprah Winfrey Show with Celine Dion in 2010, the 63rd Primetime Emmy Awards, the opening ceremonies of the 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver, and the Diamond Jubilee Show at Windsor Castle for Her Majesty Queen Elizabeth II, according to the ITV (television network). They have played in a variety of locations, including the Tel Aviv Opera House and the Mandalay Bay Events Center in Las Vegas, among others. They have also made appearances on television, including the 2009 Grey Cup broadcast and CBC Television’s Winter Festival of Ice. In the past, they have performed alongside artists such as Sarah McLachlan, Neil Young, Paul Anka, Justin Bieber, Paul McCartney, Brian McKnight, David Foster, and Natalie Cole on the same stage. Universal Music Group released their platinum-selling self-titled first album, as well as their double-platinum holiday album, The Perfect Gift, on November 30th.
