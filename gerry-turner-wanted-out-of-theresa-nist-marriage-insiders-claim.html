Gerry Turner 'Wanted Out' of Theresa Nist Marriage: Insiders Claim

The Golden Bachelor truly had us all rooting for love, proving that Cupid doesn’t check your ID before striking. But as the fairy tale of Gerry Turner and Theresa Nist unfolded, it became clear that the heartaches and hiccups of reality TV romance aren’t exclusive to the twenty-somethings.

Their romance, which went from exchanging vows on live TV back in January to announcing their split just three months later, was a testament to the idea that sometimes, the ride ends as quickly as it starts. While they put on a brave face, claiming the split was as smooth as butter, folks whispering behind the scenes — and the fact the two signed a prenup — tell a different story. According to sources nestled close to Turner’s circle, the dissolution of their marriage was anything but cordial. “Gerry and Theresa are giving up on their marriage, but they both weren’t on the same page as they’d like you to believe,” revealed an anonymous source to Life & Style.

The same source revealed that the effort to keep the flame alive seemed a bit lopsided, with Nist doing a majority of the heavy lifting. “Yes, families and distance played a part, but Theresa really loved Gerry,” the insider claimed. “She wanted to work at it more than him.” This could be why Nist’s career reportedly played a role in their split.

Nist herself voiced her frustrations regarding their stalled future plans when they announced their divorce on Good Morning America saying, “We looked at home after home, but we never got to the point where we made that decision.” The quest for a shared domicile, it seems, never quite found its footing.

Echoing Theresa, Gerry shared his take, saying their indecision stemmed from being too tied to their roots. “So we look at these conversations and we both think it’s best for the happiness of each of us to live apart,” Turner explained. Their story is a bittersweet reminder that sometimes, even with the best intentions, making a relationship work is easier said than done.
