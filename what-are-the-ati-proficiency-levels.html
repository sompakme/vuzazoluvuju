What are the ATI proficiency levels?

Answer

Overview of the ATI Proficiency Levels

There are three levels of competency on the ATI Comprehensive Predictor Examination: Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3. Prior to needing additional remediation to enhance their shortfall areas, most nursing schools demand that students achieve a least of Level 2 competency on the test.

 

So, what exactly is a level 2 in ATI?

Proficiency Level 2 is the pass mark for the test. It is the highest possible score. The first test must be taken within three (3) hours of the student failing to attain a Proficiency Level 2 or 3; he or she must then spend a minimum of three (3) hours in ATI remediation and submit a remediation plan, which must include any strengths or weaknesses, before taking the second exam.

What is the formula for calculating the ATI TEAS score?

Each question you answered correctly will be reflected on your score record alongside to the total number of questions you answered incorrectly. The number of questions on the exam portion is divided by the number of questions you answered correctly on the test part.
