Jeffrey Vandergrift missing updates DJ host of Wild 94.9's JV Show found dead after sharing Lyme

A little about 'JV'

The radio personality co-hosted the Doghouse show during the 1990s and 2000s at the station Wild 94.9.

He co-hosted the show with Dan "Elvis" Lay and Lance "Hollywood" Otani.

During the show, the trio performed stunts, prank calls, and used raunchy humor.

Radio station releases statement

Jeffrey Vandergrift also known as ‘JV’ from Wild 94.9’s “The JV Show," has been missing since Thursday, the San Francisco Police Department reported.

Friday night Wild 94.9 released a statement on his disappearance.

The statement read: "We are deeply concerned, as we know you are, that our own JV has been reported missing by the SFPD."

They added: "We're working closely with them and his wife, Natasha, who is also a dear friend and a member of the WiLD 94.9 family. We are sending love, thoughts and prayers to JV and will share more information as we receive it."

Description of Jeffrey Vandergrift

Jeffrey can best be described as a White male who is around six feet tall, 180 pounds, and bald with brown eyes and several tattoos on his arms and above his right ear.

Where he was last seen

Jeffrey Vandergrift, also known as 'JV' from Wild 94.9's "The JV Show," was last seen around 10 p.m. at his home in the 200 block of King Street, SFPD reported.

Contact info if you have information on Jeffrey's whereabouts

Anyone who sees Vandergrift is asked to call 911 and report his location and description.

People with possible information about his whereabouts can call the SFPD tip line at (415) 575-4444 or text a tip to TIP411 with "SFPD" at the start of the message.
