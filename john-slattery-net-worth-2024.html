Early Life and Career Beginnings

John Slattery was born on August 13, 1962, in Boston, Massachusetts. He developed an interest in acting at a young age and pursued his passion by studying at The Catholic University of America. Slattery began his acting career in the late 1980s, with minor roles in television series and films.

Rise to Stardom

Slattery’s breakthrough came in 2007 when he was cast as Roger Sterling in the critically acclaimed television series “Mad Men.” His performance earned him four Primetime Emmy Award nominations and two Critics’ Choice Television Awards.

Directorial Ventures

Apart from acting, Slattery has also made a name for himself as a director. He directed several episodes of “Mad Men” and made his feature film directorial debut with “God’s Pocket” in 2014.

Other Notable Roles

Slattery has also appeared in other popular TV series like “Desperate Housewives,” “Arrested Development,” and “Veep.” His film credits include “Iron Man 2,” “Ant-Man,” and “Spotlight.”
