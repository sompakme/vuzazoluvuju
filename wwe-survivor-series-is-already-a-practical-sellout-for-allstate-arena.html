WWE Survivor Series Is Already A Practical Sellout For Allstate Arena

AEW just ran All Out in Chicago, and they had nearly 2,000 tickets left when the big show started. That being said, WWE is preparing to take over a bigger venue than the NOW Arena for Survivor Series, and they are almost sold out.

WWE Survivor Series is coming to the Allstate Arena on November 25th. Wrestle Tix dropped note behind their paywall to let fans know that those tickets are almost already gone.

At this point, WWE only has 14 tickets available in their 12,943 person setup. Just under 700 tickets are in the resale market right now, so they will very likely all be snatched up by the time Survivor Series is here.

WWE Survivor Series
Sat • Nov 25 • 6:30 PM
Allstate Arena, Rosemont, IL

Available Tickets => 14
Current Setup => 12,943
Tickets Distributed => 12,891

Resale => 681

If we can back up a day, SmackDown is also selling incredibly well in the same venue. They have a bit more availability, but the company still has only less than 1,000 tickets remaining for their late-November show.

WWE Friday Night SmackDown
Fri • Nov 24 • 6:45 PM
Allstate Arena, Rosemont, IL

Available Tickets => 820
Current Setup => 12,943
Tickets Distributed => 12,085 (+88 since last week’s update)

Resale => 1,002

WWE is on a roll right now, as they bring one big event after another. Even a show like Payback, that some fans didn’t think would deliver much, was able to really pack a punch. If anything, the company has a ton of interest, and it is showing in their ticket sales.

It’s also nothing new for WWE to break records around every corner. There are lot of things that they can thank for that. In the meantime, please keep it tuned with us here at Ringside News for more on this situation and so much more.
