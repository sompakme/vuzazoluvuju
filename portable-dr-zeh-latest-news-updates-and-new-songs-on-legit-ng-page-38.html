Early Life

Portable, whose real name is Habeeb Okikiola, was born in Abeokuta, Ogun State, on March 12, 1994. He spent most of his life growing up in Ota.

Portable attended ODC and Motherâ€™s Joy Schools for his primary school education. Afterward, he attended Iganmode Grammar School at Oju Ore, Ota, Ogun State.

Music Career

Portable relocated to Lagos State from Ogun State to pursue a career in music since Lagos presented a better opportunity to break into the music industry.

In 2020, Portable dropped his first body of work, an extended playlist (EP) titled Wanted, which contained six songs.

These songs include Folohunsho, Konkobility, Olori Kori, Popoli, Soro Soro, and Sulia Kan.

In 2021, Portable featured Olamide and Poco Lee on Zazoo Zehh, which became a massive hit across Nigeria. This song was what brought Portable into the limelight.

Zazoo Zehh topped several Nigerian music charts. It debuted at number 15 on Billboard Triller Global and peaked at number 1 on Apple Music Nigeria; number 1 on iTunes Chart; and number 18 on Radio Chart.

In the first quarter of 2022, following his new-found fame brought about by the success of Zazoo Zehh, Portable dropped his second extended playlist titled Zazuu Zeh. This EP was produced by Professional Beat and there were no guest artists on any of the six tracks. The songs that make up the EP include Adura, Gasolo, My Way, Ojabo Kofo, Oro Owo, and Tambolo.

Towards the end of 2022, precisely November, Portable dropped his first studio album titled Ika of Africa.
