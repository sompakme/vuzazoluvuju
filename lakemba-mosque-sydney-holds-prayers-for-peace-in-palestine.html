Lakemba Mosque Sydney holds prayers for peace in Palestine

Australia’s largest mosque has cancelled its traditional Friday prayer and chosen instead to hold prayers in the park for peace in Palestine.

“This Friday, 20 October 2023, Lakemba Mosque will be closed for Friday Jummah only,” the mosque confirmed in a statement.

“Jummah prayers will be held at Parry Park Lakemba for peace in Palestine.”

Friday (Jummah) prayers are the main weekly worship in Islam, likened to a Sunday service in Christianity.

They would usually always be held at a mosque following a call to prayer but the Lakemba Mosque has taken the unusual step to relocate to a park this week in the name of peace.

The mosque’s decision comes as more than a dozen public protests are planned to be held in Greater Sydney over the next seven days.

A pro-Palestine march through the streets of Sydney on Saturday is expected to attract over 5000 attendees.

“We aware of at this stage up to 17 protest events not just in the city, not just in the CBD but other parts of the Sydney metropolitan area and outer areas,” NSW Police Commissioner Karen Webb told reporters on Friday.

“So it is a dynamic situation and we will be dealing with those matters (as they) become known to us.”

People would be free to exercise their right to protest peacefully and police would be working with community organisers to ensure no events were taken over by troublemakers, officers said.

The Lebanese Muslim Association, based in Lakemba, blasted PM Anthony Albanese in a statement last week for saying Israel had a right to defend itself.

“We wish to make it clear that we do not agree with the Prime Minister or indeed any politician or political party when they make a statement regarding Palestine, that ‘Israel has the right to defend itself,’” the LMA said.

The LMA went on to compare the situation in Gaza to Australia’s First Nations people.

“The LMA sees the trials and tribulations suffered by Australia’s First Nations people in the 235 years since British settlement commenced as being similar to the more than 75 years of persecution inflicted on the people of Palestine by Israel, its army and its police,” the statement read.
